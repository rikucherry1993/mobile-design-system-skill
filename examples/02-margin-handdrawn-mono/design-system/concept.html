The Trunk

There are no individuals in a forest, no separable events. The bird and the branch it sits on are a joint thing. A third or more of the food a big tree makes may go to feed other organisms.

Even different kinds of trees form partnerships. Cut down a collaborator and the others may suffer. In the great forests, the trees synchronize their masting — a network deciding, all at once, to bloom.
