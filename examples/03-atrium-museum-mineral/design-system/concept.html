Sunflowers
Vincent van Gogh
Dutch, 1853 – 1890
Origin
Arles
Date
1888
Medium
Oil on canvas

Van Gogh painted the sunflowers in a burst of optimism, awaiting Gauguin's arrival in Arles. The blooms — some fresh, some already wilting — sit against a wall of the same yellow, a study in a single colour pushed to its limit. He called the series simply la lumière: light made matter.
